INTRODUCTION

The buddhist monastic shrine of Kvabahal, popularly known as Golden Temple is one of the Nepal's most beautiful temples. it is beleived to have established by the ruler of the time, King Bhaskardeva Varma(1045-1048 AD). It is situated north of Patan Drubar Square at a distance about 200 meters on the road leading to Kunbeshvara. Although it is more commonly known in Patan as Kvabahal its Sanskrit name is Hiranyavarna Mahavihara because the main temple has gold coated roofs in Pagoda style.

Visited by thousands of devotees and world tourists, this vihara is unique in preserving tradition of the Vajrayana Buddhism. This monastery is lively and it serves as open muuseum full of important Newar Buddhiam panthen, master piece of art and scupture. Its well desciplined traditional rituals and performances are unique feature of this temple.

The principle priest of this monastery is a Baphaca(young boy) who must on no account be over twelve years old. With the assistance of an older boy or ypung man, he carries out the regular liturgical service in the main Buddha. Both priests must be members of the monastery and they serve for a period of one month, during which time they must stay inside the courtyard. The only exception to this is that twice a day the younger priest circumambulates the area where most of the monastery members live.

The youngest priest goes out for circumambulation by ringing a bell at the end of the morning service and before the evening service.

LEGENDS BEHIND THE ORIGIN OF THE GOLDEN TEMPLE

A certain queen of Marawara country Pingala by name having been disgraced by her husband, came to Guhyeshvari where she saw a vision. Here she performed penance by fasting then goddess being propitiated told her to build a Vihara and live there and to apply to her eyes a certain anjana (black ointment) which could enchant her husband.

The queen did as she was advised and placed images of gods and goddesses in the court yard of the monastery Bahalkot, shich she built and named Pingala Bahal near Pashupati now in Battisputali area. Through the power of her great penance the mind of Sadatta, the king of Marawara country became uneasy and he came here in search of his wife. While he was paying visit to Guhyeshvari shrine, he found her queen Pingala. The royal couple having appointed Buddhist priests to take care of the Pingalabahalkot, returned to their country.

In the time of the Bhaskar Deva Varma, the Buddhist priest of Pingala Bahal moved from the monastery to the other places. Then some of their descendants started living in Nyakhachok in patan. The Bhikshus of Devapattan and Chabahil came to these people and told them that they had found the image of Buddha pingala bahal and brought it to Patan.

After this they took the image to Nyakhachoke. This having been bought to the notice of the king Bhaskar Deva, he caused a new Vihara named Nhuvihara, to be built for Lord Shakyamuni Buddha. This new Vihara also begin disapproved of by the image(in the dream) the king went to ask where the image would like to be set-up. The image said he would like to be established in a place where a mouse attacked and drove away a cat. The king himself then went in search of such place and one day at a certain spot he saw a golden mouse chasing a cat. Here he built a vihara and named it “Hiranyavarna Mahavihara” i.e. the Golden Buddhist Monastery.


DEITIES IN MONASTERY

Shakyamuni Buddha

A copper image of lord Shakyamuni Buddha is installed n the ground floor of the main shrine room. It is one of the unique image of Buddha Statues fulfilling all attributes.

Shakyamuni Buddha was born in lumbini in western Nepal more than 2600 years ago. Out of compassion for humanity he taught several different means of enlightment in the form of sutra and tantra and performed innumerable activities for 45years in this sub-continent for the benefit of all sentient beings. Finally he passed away into maha Parinirvana at the age of eighty.

Prajnapatamita Scripture

A widely renowned and holy scripture called “Arya Astasahasrika Prajanaparamita Sutra” written in goldern Ranjana letter is preserved here inside the main shrine for generationsand is recited regularly by Buddhist Bajracharyas under strict rules and regulations. Since the text contains of profound wisdom teachings of lord Buddha, the recitation of this text brings blessing to the devotees abd sebtient beings in general. The nanuscript is dated Nepal samvat 345 i.e. 1215 A.D. and was written by bhikshu Ananda of Kapitanagar during the reign of Abhaya Malla.

Swyambhu Chaitya

The temple in the middle of the courtyard with the “swayambhu Chaitya” inside it is another feature, which deserves attenntion of who ever visits the temple. Surrounding the temple one can find five Buddha images and a series of twelve fine images of Lokeshvaras. According to the local legend the shrine is the oldest part of the complex older than the main shrine itself. This Swayambhu Chaitya is the Guardian deity of the members of the Buddhist community of Golden temple.

Vajrasattva Shrine

Toward the north-west corner adjoining the main temple, an image of Vajrasattva inshrined in a room, Vajrasattva is also regarded as the Primordial Buddha, the Adi-Buddha by Nepalese Buddhists. Vajrasattva is generally invoked for obscurations of the mind. His hundrede syllable mantra is very efficacious in purifying our defilements through confession practice. It is said if confession is done then non-virtuous actions or obscurations will be purified. Vajrasattva is a very popular tutelary deity for Nepalese Buddhists worship him very often through Gurumandala rite.

Manjushree Namasangiti

In the north-eastern corner of the vihara,there is a shrine room dedicated to the deity Manjushree Namasangiti established a few years ago.

Manjushree Namasangiti is one of the importanr manifestations of Manjushree, the embodiment of wisdom of all Buddhas . It is said that he was already enlighted countless aeons ago. During Shakyamuni’s time he appeared himself as one of his eight chief Bodhisattva disciples. Countless Buddhas of ten directions had beed his disciples. Therefore he has been hailed as Adi-buddha Manjushree Namasangiti text has more than eight hundred words of deep spiritual significance. The devotees of the temple recite this text at dawn everyday. Some devotees can recite the text by heart.

Amoghpash Lokeshvara

The first floor of the buiding facing to the south is Tibetab Style Monastery(gompa) where one finds a beautiful image of “Amoghpash Lokeshvara” flanked by standing Green tara and white tara. It is eight armed form of Avalokiteshvara that seems to have been popular in Nepal since the Middle Ages. The name suggests that he is the lord of world, who leads suffering sentient beings to enlightment.

Newar Buddhists both laymen and women-perform Upsadha and Silent fasting on special days of Full moon and Half moon days. Tibetan Buddhist Master very oftern preach the Buddhist teaching at regular intervals. There it seems that this shrine serves as the centre for Tibetan Buddhist teaching on Newar Community.

Tara Shrine

On the southern side of the vihara and on its ground floor, there are two fine images of Bodhisattva Tara and Manjushree.

Tara represents the entire enlightened activities of all Buddhas and hence she is called the mother of the Buddhas. There are 21 forms of tara. In reality, they are all the transformed bodies of Avalokiteshvara, the Bodhisattva of compassion.

Green Tara appeared as young beautiful maiden she is quick to answer the prayers of those who seek her aid. Tara is known as the great liberator from eight kinds of danger or fears. The cultivation of tara Sadhana will eliminate all demoniac and karmic obstacles, eradicates disaster and lengthen one’s life span. In this Tara Shrine the devotees performs Bhajans (singing songs) dedicated to buddhas and Bodhisattvas.

Amitabha Buddha with his two Bodhisattvas

In the first floor of the southern building there is a permanent exhibit of large size images of Buddha Dipankhara. A huge image of Buddha Amitabha flanked by two attendant bodhisattvas namely Padmapani and Mahasthamprapta is worth seeing.

Amitabha in Sanskrit means immeasurable light. He resides in the western land of unlimited bliss (skt.Sukhavati). When he was a Bodhisattva he was called Bhikshu Dharmakara. He made 48 vows to establish an adorned land of unlimited bliss to ferry over those living being across the world by reciting his name. On the basic of those vows, any living being who has faith, makes vows and practices diligently, will be received by this Buddha and the devotees will be reborn in the pure land of bliss.

With his extensive vows and great compassion, this Buddha has ferried over innumerable sentient beings. The recitation of the name of Amitabha Buddha is a common practice in China and Japan. In Tibet too, Devotees recite very often the prayer to be reborn on land of Amitabha Buddha. In Japan also Amitabha Buddhas invisibly keeps guarding the devotees against danger and death.

Yogambara

In the first floor of the eastern side of the vihara there is a Vijrayanic shrine dedicated to the deity Yogambara. Yogambara is one of the most popular Tutelary deity of Nepalese Buddhists especially of Kvabahal Sangha community. Only the members initiated in Vajrayanic tradition can enter into this shrine. This special temple is attended by Chakresvara, the senior most elder of the Buddhist Sangha of this monastery. The members of the sangha come to this shrine to perform tantric puja and ganacakra puja on the occasion of Cudadarma(ordination) and Acaluyegu(master initiation). This shrine is strictly prohibited for foreign visitors.

Mahakala

In the ground of eastern part of the vihara there is the stone image of the deity Mahakala fixed on wall. Mahakala is the deity worshipped both by Hindus and the Buddhists. He can be seen in the entrance of every Buddhist monasteries of Kathmandu vally.

Image of Lokeshvara

The four images of Lokeshvara is standing position places in all four corners of the Courtyard are considered to be of great significance. It is because of their superb artistic value that these four Bodhi Sattvas are very well preserved.


LOCATION

Hiraṇyavarṇa Mahāvihāra (Nepali: हिरण्यवर्ण महाविहार), also Kwa Bahal (Nepali: क्वबहा:) informally referred to as the The Golden Temple with literal meaning "Gold-colored Great Monastery" is a historical vihara (Buddhist monastery) situated in Patan, Nepal.It is known locally as the "Golden Temple" and it is famous for feeding rats.
